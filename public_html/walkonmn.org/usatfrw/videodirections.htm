Directions for Creating the Video

for the video coaching program

 

 

The best video is captured under race conditions, but sometimes this is not practical. A reasonable

substitute is to video a workout that mimics race length and speed as closely as possible. An indoor or outdoor track is a good place to capture video because the lap size is relatively small, providing many opportunities to video an athlete within a single workout.

 

Be sure to video at the appropriate stage in the race/workout.  Early stages may capture warm-up issues. Middle stages may capture the best form. Late stages may capture fatigue issues.

 

If you are videoing during a race, or on a track with other athletes, be careful to not interfere with the other athletes. Creating the video is important, but your athlete will come around again on the next lap, so any one opportunity is not that significant.

 

Capture video in a straight part of the course, not at a corner, unless the athlete has a particular issue with corners.

 

There are 4 views.

 

Front View

The front view of the athlete is captured by stepping onto the course when the athlete is a long way away and videoing until the athlete more than fills the frame of the video camera, then stepping off the course before the athlete walks over you.

 

Back View

The back view of the athlete is captured by stepping onto the course immediately after the athlete goes by and videoing until the athlete is very small in the frame.

 

Right Side View

The right side view of the athlete is captured by standing on the right side (as viewed by the athlete) of the course, at least 15 feet away from the path the athlete will follow. Select a zoom setting so the athlete "fills the frame" when they walk by. If the athlete more than fills the frame at the lowest zoom level, then back away from the path. It is best to find the spot to stand before you are under pressure of videoing the athlete. Start videoing as the athlete approaches, as the athlete passes, and for just a little bit as the athlete goes away.

 

Left Side View

The left side view of the athlete is captured by standing on the left side (as viewed by the athlete) of the course, but otherwise exactly like the right side view.

 

Technique Coaching

These directions are for creating the standard video for technique coaching. If you have an ongoing relationship with a technique coach, the coach may request something different.

 

Create a video with each view in each stage of the race/workout (early, middle, late). If possible, capture a couple more side views in each stage. If you are skilled enough to zoom while videoing, make the athlete "fills the frame".

 

Judging

Create a video with 2 right side views and 2 left side views in each stage of the race/workout (early, middle, late). Start videoing each view when the walker is quite far away. Do not adjust the zoom.
